KAYANGAN

LAKE

Kayangan Lake is a brackish lake located on Coron Island, Palawan, Philippines, renowned for being one of the cleanest lakes in the country. The lake is sacred to the indigenous Tagbanwa people, who historically believed nature spirits inhabited it. Kayangan Lake is a top destination for swimming, snorkeling, and photography. A viewing deck offers panoramic views, making it one of the most photographed spots in the Philippines. .

TRAVEL TIP

Swimming is permitted in designated areas and the water’s exceptional clarity makes it ideal for snorkeling. Most visitors highlight the breathtaking scenery and well-maintained trails as major positives, though some note that the climb may be challenging for those with mobility issues. The lake is part of a protected area, so environmental guidelines are strictly enforced to preserve its natural state.

WHAT TO EXPECT

Visitors describe Kayangan Lake as breathtaking and worth the climb, with crystal-clear turquoise waters and dramatic limestone cliffs that match or exceed expectations from photos. The hike involves over 300 steps up and down, but the panoramic viewpoint and scenic trail make it rewarding.

DIVE WITH US

GUIDE

Visiting Kayangan Lake requires a licensed local guide, who plays a vital role in the experience. Guides are mandatory for safety and cultural respect, as the lake is sacred to the Tagbanwa indigenous people.
