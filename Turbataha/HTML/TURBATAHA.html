TUBBATAHA REEFS

NATURAL PARK DIVING

SPOT

Tubbataha Reefs Natural Parkis a breathtaking UNESCO World Heritage Site located in the heart of the Sulu Sea. It features two large atolls—the North and South Atolls—and the smaller Jessie Beazley Reef, all teeming with vibrant corals, sea turtles, sharks, and over 600 species of fish. Known as one of the best diving spots in the world, the park offers crystal-clear waters and an unspoiled underwater paradise. Its remote location and pristine ecosystem make it a true haven for marine life and a must-visit destination for divers and ocean enthusiasts. .

TRAVEL TIP

Tubbataha Reefs Natural Park can only be reached through liveaboard boats from Puerto Princesa, usually lasting five to seven nights. There are no resorts or day trips, so plan ahead and book at least nine to twelve months in advance since slots fill up fast. A Marine Park fee is required and typically included in your liveaboard package. Divers must follow strict park rules to protect the reef—so bring your certification card, reef-safe sunscreen, and a sense of adventure .

WHAT TO EXPECT

In Tubbataha, divers encounter remarkable reef walls and vibrant coral reefs while being surrounded by an array of marine life like colorful fish, sharks, manta rays (elm trees) and turtles. The clear waters here can be seen in great detail.

DIVE WITH US

GUIDE

From mid-March to mid-June, the sea is calm and weather conditions are ideal. During this period, underwater visibility can reach up to 30 meters, offering perfect conditions for diving and underwater photography. The dry and sunny weather also ensures a smooth and enjoyable liveaboard experience. Outside this season, the park is closed due to rough seas and unpredictable weather brought by the southwest monsoon.
