PORT BARTON BEACH VILLAGE IN SAN

VICENTE TOWN

Port Barton, located in the municipality of San Vicente, Palawan, is a charming fishing village turned relaxed backpacker haven known for its beautiful, less crowded beaches and island-hopping tours. Visitors enjoy snorkeling, diving, and visiting nearby islands like White Beach as well as exploring local waterfalls and the surrounding mountains. It offers a peaceful, laid-back atmosphere with beachfront dining, bars, and opportunities to experience local life.

Travel Tips

Bring cash since ATMs are limited, and expect spotty mobile signal—making it a great place for a true digital detox. Wear reef-safe sunscreen, respect local marine life, and pack light but essential items like a power bank, reusable water bottle, and insect repellent for a comfortable and eco-friendly trip.

WHAT TO EXPECT

It retains a "sleepy haven" feel, with limited streetlights at night and a generally less crowded experience than more tourist-heavy areas. Despite its size, the village has a handful of good restaurants, cafés, and beach bars. However, keep in mind that Wi-Fi can be unreliable, and some accommodations may have inconsistent electricity or running water. The town is an ideal place to unwind, relax on the beach, and enjoy the tranquil pace. 

Guide

The easiest way is to fly into Puerto Princesa International Airport, then travel by van or private transfer to San Vicente town (4–5 hours). Overland transfers are also available from El Nido town (3–4 hours). San Vicente town has its own airport, but there are currently no commercial flights operating. The second option for getting to Port Barton from Puerto Princesa is renting a car. Because the airport is an international airport, there are cars available for hire. However, this tends to be expensive and is only worth it if you have a whole road trip around Palawan planned out.

WHAT TO BRING

Swimwear, Towel, a change of Clothes

Sunscreen, Hat, and Sunglasses

Aqua Shoes

Waterproof camera or Phone Case

Cash for fees and optional rentals

Reusable water bottle, eco-bag

BEST TIME TO GO

The best time to visit San Vicente is during the dry season, from December to April, extending up to mid-May. These months bring plenty of sunshine, calm seas, and ideal conditions for swimming, snorkeling, and island-hopping. Thanks to its location on the western side of Palawan Island, San Vicente is less prone to typhoons than provinces in eastern parts of the Philippines. This period also coincides with San Vicente town’s peak season, especially in Port Barton village, where many international visitors—particularly from Europe—flock to escape their winter months.

ACTIVITIES

Port Barton, beloved for its island-hopping adventures, snorkeling spots, and relaxed, small-town vibe. Combining tranquility with adventure, San Vicente town’s peaceful fishing villages, uncrowded beaches, and eco-tourism charm make it perfect for travelers seeking a more laid-back side of Palawan Island. Include exploring Long Beach, joining island-hopping tours, visiting inland attractions like Bato ni Ningning viewpoint, and snorkeling in vibrant reefs with sea turtles.

ACCOMODATION

Port Barton offers a range of lodging options, from hostels to locally run resorts and boutique hotels, many of which are budget-friendly. Popular options include beachfront cottages and some area nearby offers accommodation like homestays and guest houses.

~~~~~~~~~~~~LOCAL DISH~~~~~~~~~~~~

Food focuses on fresh seafood, including grilled fish and kinilaw (a raw seafood salad), along with Filipino staples like adobo, lechon kawali (crispy pork belly), and nido soup (bird's nest soup). Tourists can enjoy these local flavors at local restaurants and during island-hopping tours, which often include fresh seafood barbecue.

FUN FACT

Port Barton, a peaceful village in San Vicente, Palawan, is often called Palawan’s “hidden gem” for its quiet beaches, stunning sunsets, and relaxed island vibe. Named after an English naval officer, this charming spot remains less crowded than El Nido or Puerto Princesa, making it perfect for travelers who want to unwind and reconnect with nature. You can spot sea turtles near the shore, enjoy island-hopping to nearby islets, or simply watch the sky turn golden at sunset.
