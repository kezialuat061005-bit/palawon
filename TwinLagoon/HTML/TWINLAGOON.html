TWIN LAGOON

IN

CORON TOWN

Twin Lagoon in Coron Town is one of Palawan’s most enchanting natural wonders, famous for its stunning mix of emerald and turquoise waters surrounded by towering limestone cliffs. The name “Twin Lagoon” comes from the two bodies of water separated by a dramatic limestone wall—visitors can either swim through a narrow crevice or climb a short wooden ladder to pass between them. The outer lagoon is accessible by boat, while the inner lagoon feels like a hidden sanctuary, offering calm, crystal-clear waters perfect for swimming and floating. What makes it extra fascinating is the unique thermocline effect—when warm saltwater from the sea meets cooler freshwater from underground springs, creating layers of varying temperatures as you swim. .

TRAVEL TIP

One of the best tourist spots in Coron town, Twin Lagoon shines as a true natural marvel. Its unique dual-basin formation—two serene lagoons separated by a rugged limestone barrier with a narrow passage—creates a rare phenomenon that only reveals itself at low tide. This hidden gateway invites you to swim between two distinct aquatic realms, each boasting its own captivating charm and ecological treasures, making Twin Lagoon one of the best attractions in Palawan Island.

WHAT TO EXPECT

Your island hopping adventure to Twin Lagoon begins with a scenic boat ride from Coron town, where local guides provide a safety briefing and insider tips on exploring the area. As you approach Twin Lagoon, the brilliant turquoise waters and dramatic limestone formations immediately captivate your senses At low tide, the narrow natural passage opens, offering the once-in-a-lifetime experience of swimming from one tranquil lagoon.
