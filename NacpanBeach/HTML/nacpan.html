NACPAN BEACH

IN EL NIDO

Nacpan Beach is a 4-kilometer stretch of powdery cream-to-golden sand lined with swaying coconut trees and bordered by clear turquoise waters, located about 17 km north of El Nido town in Palawan, Philippines, and often celebrated as one of the country’s most beautiful beaches. Known as one of El Nido’s "Twin Beaches" alongside Calitang Beach, it combines rustic charm with breathtaking natural scenery, including panoramic views from the hill separating the two beaches.

Travel Tips

Nacpan offers a relaxed alternative to island hopping. Highlights include sunset views, kayaking, paddleboarding, and hiking the hill between Nacpan and Calitang Beach for panoramic views.

WHAT TO EXPECT

A 4-km stretch of powdery sand, turquoise waters, and coconut trees. Despite popularity, it remains uncrowded.

Guide

Distance from El Nido town: ~17 km Travel time: 20–45 minutes by motorbike, tricycle, or van Transport options: Tricycle, motorbike rental, private van, or shuttle (round-trip shuttle: ~600 PHP) From El Nido Airport (Lio): ~11.5 miles, 23 minutes by taxi (₱350–470)

WHAT TO BRING

Swimwear, Towel, a change of Clothes

Sunscreen, Hat, and Sunglasses

Aqua Shoes

Waterproof camera or Phone Case

Cash for fees and optional rentals

Reusable water bottle, eco-bag

BEST TIME TO GO

November to May (dry season) offers calm seas and sunny weather. Visit in the late afternoon for stunning sunsets.

ACTIVITIES

Kayaking is the main highlight at Big Lagoon, letting you paddle through serene emerald waters surrounded by majestic limestone cliffs. You can also swim in certain shallow parts, snorkel near the entrance, or take stunning drone and GoPro shots for that picture-perfect travel memory. Many visitors enjoy combining Big Lagoon with nearby destinations like Small Lagoon, Secret Lagoon, and Shimizu Island as part of their island-hopping adventure. For a more relaxed pace, simply float and take in the stillness of nature—a moment of calm that perfectly captures what Palawan is all about.

ACCOMODATION

Options range from budget hostels and beach huts to boutique resorts and glamping sites. Staying near El Nido town offers more dining and nightlife options, while accommodations in Nacpan provide serene beachfront access.

~~~~~~~~~~~~LOCAL DISH~~~~~~~~~~~~

Enjoy fresh seafood, Filipino classics like adobo and sinigang, and tropical fruit shakes at beachfront eateries. Notable spots include Squidos and Sea Slugs for seafood.

FUN FACT

Nacpan Beach in El Nido is one of Palawan’s most stunning and peaceful coastal gems, stretching around four kilometers of soft, powdery white sand. What makes it extra special is that it forms the famous “Twin Beach” with Calitang Beach, where the two shorelines meet at a small hill that offers a breathtaking panoramic view of both sides—an ideal spot for photography and sunsets. Despite gaining popularity among travelers, Nacpan still retains its tranquil charm, with long stretches of sand where you can walk for minutes without seeing another person. The beach beautifully balances rustic simplicity with comfort, offering a mix of cozy glamping sites, small boutique resorts, and beachside cottages while keeping its natural vibe intact. The area around Nacpan Beach is surrounded by tall coconut palms and lush greenery, creating a true tropical paradise setting. Many visitors enjoy stargazing at night, as the absence of city lights makes the sky sparkle beautifully on clear evenings. The gentle waves and clean, shallow waters make it perfect for swimming and relaxing by the shore. For those seeking a bit of adventure, climbing the hill that separates Nacpan and Calitang Beaches rewards you with one of the most unforgettable views in El Nido. Whether you’re there to unwind, explore, or simply admire nature, Nacpan Beach offers a serene escape that captures the very essence of island life in Palawan.
