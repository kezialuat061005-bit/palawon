SHIPWRECK DIVING

SPOTS

IN CORON TOWn

Shipwreck Diving Spots in Coron Town are world-famous underwater sites that blend history and adventure. These sunken Japanese ships from World War II now rest beneath Coron Bay, transformed into stunning dive sites teeming with corals and marine life. Divers of all levels can explore different wrecks—from shallow ones like Lusong Gunboat to deeper dives such as Akitsushima and Irako. Each site offers a unique glimpse into the past, making Coron a must-visit destination for both history buffs and underwater explorers .

TRAVEL TIP

 All the dive action starts from Coron Town Proper, so that’s where most visitors stay. You’ll find everything from cozy backpacker hostels to comfortable dive resorts, most within walking distance of dive shops and restaurants.The wreck sites themselves are spread across Coron Bay, about 30 minutes to an hour away by boat, so day trips from town are the easiest way to explore them. 

WHAT TO EXPECT

Beyond the fascinating wrecks, Coron’s underwater world is bursting with life. Expect to see schools of jackfish, lionfish, and batfish weaving through rusted beams, while soft corals and sponges bloom across the decks.The mix of history and thriving marine ecosystems makes each dive feel like exploring a living museum.

DIVE WITH US

GUIDE

You’ll always dive with a licensed local guide or instructor, which is both a safety requirement and a bonus. They know the stories behind each wreck, the safest routes through the hulls, and where to find hidden marine life like lionfish or nudibranchs. Their knowledge really brings the dives to life.
