ONOK ISLAND

IN BALABAC

MUNICIPALITY

Onok Island is a hidden paradise in the southernmost part of Palawan often called the “Maldives of the Philippines.” Surrounded by turquoise waters, powdery white sand, and rich coral reefs, this private island offers a peaceful escape far from the noise of the city. It’s one of the most beautiful yet less-traveled destinations in the country, where time seems to slow down, and nature takes center stage.

TRAVEL TIP

Getting to Onok Island is an adventure on its own it takes a mix of land, sea, and sometimes small boat rides, but the journey is worth it! Always coordinate your visit with the local tourism office in Balabac since the island is privately owned and requires permission to enter. Bring your essentials like sunscreen, snacks, and enough water, as facilities on the island are limited.

WHAT TO EXPECT

Expect peace, beauty, and pure island life. Onok Island is surrounded by crystal-clear lagoons where you can swim, snorkel, or simply float and relax. You’ll see colorful fish, sea turtles, and vibrant corals just a few steps from the shore.

DIVE WITH US

GUIDE

Travel Time: About 4–5 hours from Puerto Princesa to Rio Tuba by land, then another 2–3 hours by boat to Balabac and Onok Island. Permits: Coordinate with Balabac tourism or the island caretakers before your trip.
