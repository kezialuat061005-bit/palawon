HONDA BAY ISLAND IN PUERTO PRINCESA

CITY

Honda Bay is one of Palawan’s most beautiful and accessible island destinations. Just a short drive from Puerto Princesa City, it offers clear turquoise waters, white sandy beaches, and vibrant marine life perfect for snorkeling and swimming. Each island from Cowrie to Luli to Starfish Island has its own charm and beauty.

Travel Tips

Honda Bay is best explored early in the morning when the sea is calm and the sun is bright. Book your island-hopping tour in advance and don’t forget essentials like sunscreen, water, and snorkeling gear. Bring some cash for entrance fees and snacks since most islands don’t accept cards.

WHAT TO EXPECT

Expect stunning beaches, friendly locals, and clear waters filled with colorful fish. Each island offers something different from floating cottages to snorkeling spots and picture-perfect beaches.

Guide

Most tours include hotel pickup, boat transfers, lunch, and snorkeling gear. You’ll visit 3–4 islands in one day. Entrance fees are small, and the experience is absolutely worth it.

WHAT TO BRING

Swimwear, Towel, a change of Clothes

Sunscreen, Hat, and Sunglasses

Aqua Shoes

Waterproof camera or Phone Case

Cash for fees and optional rentals

Reusable water bottle, eco-bag

BEST TIME TO GO

The best time to visit Honda Bay is between November and May, during Palawan’s dry season. The skies are clear, the sea is calm, and the islands are glowing with sunshine. The rainy season (June to October) can bring rough waves and limited tours, so plan your trip during the dry months for the best experience.

ACTIVITIES

Go island hopping, snorkeling, kayaking, or simply relax by the beach. Don’t miss the fresh seafood lunches and picture-perfect views every moment in Honda Bay feels like paradise.

ACCOMODATION

Stay in Puerto Princesa City, where you’ll find hotels and resorts for every budget. It’s just 20–30 minutes away from the Honda Bay port, making it easy and convenient for travelers.

~~~~~~~~~~~~LOCAL DISH~~~~~~~~~~~~

A trip to Honda Bay isn’t complete without tasting Palawan’s local flavors! Enjoy grilled seafood, fresh crabs, and kinilaw (Filipino-style ceviche) prepared by locals on the islands. Try the Tamilok, a famous Palawan delicacy made from woodworms found in mangroves it’s not for everyone, but it’s an unforgettable experience! You can also enjoy tropical fruits like mangoes, coconuts, and bananas served fresh and sweet.

FUN FACT

The name Honda means “deep” in Spanish fitting for a bay known for its rich marine life. Some islands, like Luli, got their names from the phrase Lulubog-Lilitaw (sink and appear) because they vanish during high tide! Bring only reef-safe sunscreen to protect the corals. Always wear a life vest during boat rides and snorkeling activities. Stay hydrated and reapply sunscreen often the Palawan sun can be intense! Respect marine life avoid touching corals or starfish. Keep the islands clean. Bring your trash back to the boat and help preserve the bay’s beauty.
