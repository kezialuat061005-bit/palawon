SMALL LAGOON

IN

EL NIDO PALAWAN

Small Lagoon is one of the more intimate lagoons in Miniloc Island, El Nido. It’s surrounded by impressive limestone cliffs and hidden behind a narrow opening in the rock—boats can’t enter this gap, so visitors must swim or use kayaks to pass through. Inside, the water is calm, crystal-clear, and sheltered, which makes for an enchanting atmosphere of peace and natural beauty. It’s included in Island Hopping Tour A, which runs from about 9 AM to 4 PM and typically includes boat, guide, life vests, snorkeling gear, and lunch.

TRAVEL TIP

To make the most of your visit to Small Lagoon, go early in the morning to avoid the crowds and enjoy its peaceful atmosphere. Confirm with your tour operator if kayak rentals are included, and wear aqua shoes since the entrance rocks can be sharp. Check the weather and tide before your trip, as low tide can make entry tricky. Bring only essentials like a waterproof camera, dry bag, and water, and don’t forget reef-safe sunscreen. Most importantly, respect the environment—avoid littering and touching the rocks or corals to help preserve this natural paradise.

WHAT TO EXPECT

Visitors describe Kayangan Lake as breathtaking and worth the climb, with crystal-clear turquoise waters and dramatic limestone cliffs that match or exceed expectations from photos. The hike involves over 300 steps up and down, but the panoramic viewpoint and scenic trail make it rewarding.

DIVE WITH US

GUIDE

Visiting Kayangan Lake requires a licensed local guide, who plays a vital role in the experience. Guides are mandatory for safety and cultural respect, as the lake is sacred to the Tagbanwa indigenous people.
