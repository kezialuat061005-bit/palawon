MALCAPUYA ISLAND

IN CORON TOWN

Malcapuya Island in Coron Town is a breathtaking tropical escape known for its powdery white sand, crystal-clear waters, and peaceful atmosphere. Located about 1.5 hours by boat from Coron proper, it’s a favorite stop for island-hopping tours and offers the perfect spot for swimming, snorkeling, or simply relaxing under the palm trees. With its calm waves and vibrant marine life, Malcapuya is often called a “hidden paradise” for those seeking quiet beauty away from the crowds.

Travel Tips

To make the most of your visit, join an organized island-hopping tour that includes Malcapuya Island for convenience and safety. Be mindful of the environment by avoiding littering and using reef-safe sunscreen to protect marine life. Take time to explore both the beach and the viewpoints for a panoramic look at the island’s stunning coastline—an experience that perfectly captures the natural charm of Coron.

WHAT TO EXPECT

Malcapuya Island is known for its serene atmosphere, soft white sand, and reef-protected shores. The waters here are typically calm, perfect for swimming or simply relaxing by the beach with a good book. On a clear day, the view stretches across the sea, offering a peaceful ambiance that feels worlds away from the hustle and bustle of daily life. Snorkeling enthusiasts will find coral reefs just offshore teeming with tropical fish. For those who enjoy a bit of adventure, a short hike up a nearby hill reveals panoramic views of the surrounding islets. This laid-back destination is often considered a highlight of Coron island hopping tours.

Guide

To visit Malcapuya Island, travelers can book daily flights to Coron (via Busuanga Airport). From there, you can arrange convenient Coron airport transfers to your hotel or directly to the boat port where island hopping tours depart. Malcapuya Island is about 1.5 to 2 hours by boat from Coron town, depending on sea conditions. The easiest way to visit is by booking a tour to Coron's best beaches or Tour C, which includes Malcapuya Island, along with other beach destinations in the area. Top tours in Coron town can also be part of curated packages.

WHAT TO BRING

Swimwear, Towel, a change of Clothes

Sunscreen, Hat, and Sunglasses

Aqua Shoes

Waterproof camera or Phone Case

Cash for fees and optional rentals

Reusable water bottle, eco-bag

BEST TIME TO GO

The best time to visit Malcapuya Island is during the dry season, from November to May, when skies are clear, seas are calm, and island hopping conditions are ideal. Be sure to bring sunscreen, swimwear, and a waterproof bag to make the most of your day at the beach.

ACTIVITIES

Visitors can enjoy swimming, snorkeling, and kayaking in Malcapuya’s crystal-clear waters. You can also explore the coral gardens nearby, take a short hike up the hill for a stunning panoramic view of the island, or simply relax under the coconut trees. Photography enthusiasts will love capturing the island’s postcard-perfect scenery, especially during sunset. For those seeking a bit of fun, beach volleyball and frisbee are popular among groups.

ACCOMODATION

Overnight stays are not permitted on Malcapuya Island, as it’s a protected site meant for day tours. However, there are many great places to stay in Coron Town, just a short boat ride away. You can choose from budget-friendly inns and guesthouses to luxury beachfront resorts such as Two Seasons Coron Bayside Hotel, The Funny Lion, and Busuanga Bay Lodge. Many hotels also help arrange tours or private boat hires that include Malcapuya, Banana, and Bulog Dos Islands.

~~~~~~~~~~~~LOCAL DISH~~~~~~~~~~~~

While there are no restaurants on the island, most island-hopping tours include a freshly prepared lunch — often grilled fish, chicken, rice, and tropical fruits. Back in Coron Town, you can enjoy local favorites such as lato (sea grapes), kinilaw (Filipino ceviche), and grilled seafood platters, all showcasing Palawan’s coastal flavors.

FUN FACT

Malcapuya Island is privately owned but open to the public for day tours. It’s part of the Calamian Islands group and is often dubbed the “Boracay of the North” because of its fine white sand and clear blue waters. Interestingly, even though it’s more remote, Malcapuya remains one of the cleanest and most well-preserved beaches in Palawan, thanks to the community’s and local guides’ efforts to protect its natural beauty.
