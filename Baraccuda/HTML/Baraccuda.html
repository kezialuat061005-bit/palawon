BARRACUDA LAKE

IN CORON

Located in Coron Town on Busuanga Island, Barracuda Lake is easily reachable from the island's location within the Calamian Islands in Palawan. The serene lake, which boasts both freshwater and brackish waters and is surrounded by magnificent limestone cliffs, has made it a popular destination for freedivers and divers. Coron island-hopping tours often include this surreal and scenic experience, which is perfect for anyone looking for a thrill.

Travel Tips

The arrival at Barracuda Lake is marked by the impressive limestone formations and clear, crystal-clear water. Witness unique underwater phenomena as cooler freshwater sits on top of warmer layers, creating visually captivating visual effects from thermoclines and haloclines.

WHAT TO EXPECT

The waters of Barracuda Lake are incredibly calm, making them ideal for swimming and freediving. Water temperatures undergo a significant shift, with cool layers cooling off and reaching higher depths. Despite the fact that marine biodiversity is lower than at most diving sites, smaller fish, shrimps, and occasional barracuda can be observed among the stunning underwater scenery.

Guide

The way to reach Coron Town is by flying into Busuanga Airport (USU) from Manila or Cebu, then taking a flight. Take a boat trip to Barracuda Lake from there; a flight of 50-60 minutes is anticipated, followed by a 20-30 minute boat ride to the lake. The cost of admission for each person is roughly PHP 200, with an additional fee of PHP 500 for hiring a boat or tour guide. Prices for shared tours typically range from $30 to $60 per person.

WHAT TO BRING

Swimwear, Towel, a change of Clothes

Sunscreen, Hat, and Sunglasses

Aqua Shoes

Waterproof camera or Phone Case

Cash for fees and optional rentals

Reusable water bottle, eco-bag

BEST TIME TO GO

The best time to visit Barracuda Lake is during the dry season, which typically runs from October to May. During these months, you can expect excellent weather conditions, with lower humidity and minimal rainfall, making it ideal for swimming, snorkeling, and exploring the lake's fascinating underwater landscapes. If you aim to avoid the crowds, visiting during the shoulder months of October and May can offer a more tranquil experience, allowing you to fully appreciate the natural beauty of the area. Regardless of when you visit, it’s essential to be prepared for the sun by bringing adequate sun protection and staying hydrated, as the tropical weather can be intense.

ACTIVITIES

At Barracuda Lake, you can experience exciting adventures both above and below the water. Try freediving or scuba diving (for certified divers) to explore the lake’s fascinating underwater thermoclines and cave-like rock formations. You can also enjoy snorkeling in the crystal-clear waters, swimming in its calm and refreshing depths, or simply relaxing while admiring the surrounding limestone cliffs. Don’t forget to bring your camera to capture the breathtaking beauty of this unique natural wonder.

ACCOMODATION

In Coron Town, there are many lodging options available such as budget-friendly inns and guesthouses, as well as mid-range resorts or high-end accommodations. Many options are available, including private boat tours and liveaboards, but overnight stays on the lake are not permitted.

~~~~~~~~~~~~LOCAL DISH~~~~~~~~~~~~

The food scene in Barracuda Lake and its surrounding areas is characterized by the dish known as "Barracudas." This local dish is highly prized. Fresh marine products are reflected in the region's cuisine, with barracuda caught locally being cooked and served as a marinade or marinated fish. Beyond that, you may come across a widely popular dish named "Lato", or sea grapes; this edible seaweed is often served with salads or as supplementary dishes and serves to highlight Palawan's coastal culinary heritage.

FUN FACT

Barracuda Lake got its name from a large barracuda skeleton discovered in its depths. What makes it truly unique is the mix of freshwater and saltwater, where temperatures can shift dramatically from 28°C to 38°C as you dive deeper — creating a fascinating experience for divers. When visiting, always follow your guide’s instructions and wear a life vest when swimming. Be cautious of sharp rocks and ensure your gear is waterproof. Most importantly, respect the environment by not littering or touching marine life, so this stunning natural site remains beautiful for future visitors.
